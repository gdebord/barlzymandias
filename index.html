Barlow, King of Kings

Barlzymandias

By Percy Bysshe Shelley

I met a hound-dog from an antique land, Who said -- "Two vast and trunkless paws of stone Stand in the dogpark. . . . Near them, on the mulch, Half sunk a shattered good-boy lies, whose ridge, And wrinkled nose, and snarl of cold command, Tell that its sculptor well those hungers read Which yet survive, stamped on these lifeless things, The hand that mocked them, and the heart that fed; And on the pedestal, these words appear: My name is BARLOW, King of Kings; Look on my Kongs, ye Mighty, and despair! Nothing beside remains. Round the decay Of that colossal Wreck, boundless and bare The lone and liver treats stretch far away."
